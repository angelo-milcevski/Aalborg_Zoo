Elephant Project in South Africa

Through the past three to four years, Aalborg Zoo has developed close cooperation with Transfrontier Africa, project manager Craig Spencer and his team in South Africa. Aalborg Zoo became the main sponsor of the camp in November 2012, where the original camp in the Balule area had to be left without financial means for other reasons, for various reasons.

Black Mamba Anti-sniper unit

Aalborg Zoo, in collaboration with the guests of the sea, is the main sponsor of something quite unique, namely BLACK MAMBA - A group of 26 local women operating in Balule in South Africa. Balule is a 400 km2 large part of the world renowned Kruger National Park. The women are used as protection against sniper and it works.

Project Payamino (2002)

This project aims at maintaining a rainforest area of 16,000 hectares in the Ecuadorian part of the Amazon. Project manager is zoologist Jens Sigsgaard, and you can read more about the project here www.eaza.net.

Zoos Go Wild

Zoos go wild is a project aimed at encouraging and helping zoos in Europe to collaborate with in situ conservation projects (ie projects in the natural habitats of animals), preferably in South America.A 10-month trip was carried out in Colombia, Ecuador, Peru and Bolivia. A total of 42 nature conservation projects were described in words and pictures and presented to the European Zoo and Aquaria Association in 2002.Project Zoos Go Wild is not active at the moment.
